# Quality Criteria: Milestones & Gateways
Effective milestones and gateways are essential for tracking project progress and ensuring that quality standards are met at each stage of development. This document provides a structured template and quality criteria for defining, evaluating, and maintaining milestones and gateways, referencing best practices from "Applying UML and patterns by Craig Larman".

## Quality Criteria
When evaluating milestones and gateways, consider the following quality criteria:
1. **Clarity and Specificity**: Milestones and gateways must be clearly defined with specific objectives and deliverables. Avoid vague descriptions that can lead to confusion.
1. **Relevance**: Milestones and gateways should be relevant to the project's goals and objectives, ensuring that they contribute to the overall success of the project.
1. **Measurability**: Milestones and gateways should have measurable criteria to assess progress and performance. This allows for objective evaluation and decision-making.
1. **Achievability**: Milestones and gateways should be realistic and attainable within the project's constraints, including time, resources, and budget.
1. **Timeliness**: Milestones and gateways should be scheduled appropriately to ensure timely progress and to identify potential issues early.
1. **Traceability**: Milestones and gateways should be traceable to specific project requirements, objectives, or deliverables, ensuring alignment with the project's goals.
1. **Documentation**: Milestones and gateways should be well-documented, including descriptions, criteria, and any relevant supporting information.
1. **Stakeholder Validation**: Milestones and gateways should be reviewed and validated by relevant stakeholders to ensure they meet the project's needs and expectations.
1. **Versioning and Change Log**: Every change to milestones and gateways must be logged with a version, date, and author to maintain a clear history of modifications.
1. **Language**: Milestones and gateways should be documented in a clear and consistent language, ensuring that all stakeholders can understand and interpret the information accurately.
